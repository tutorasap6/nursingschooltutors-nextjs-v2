### Practice Multiple-Choice Questions

**Question 1:** A nurse is caring for a patient with advanced heart failure. Which of the following assessment findings requires immediate intervention?

A) Bilateral crackles in the lung bases  
B) 1+ pitting edema in bilateral lower extremities  
C) Oxygen saturation of 84% on room air  
D) Blood pressure of 98/60 mmHg

**✅ Correct Answer: C**

**Rationale:** An oxygen saturation of 84% on room air represents severe hypoxemia and requires immediate nursing intervention, including repositioning, supplemental oxygen administration, and notification of the healthcare provider. While bilateral crackles (A) indicate fluid accumulation and pitting edema (B) suggests volume overload, these are expected findings in heart failure. The blood pressure (D) should be monitored but does not represent an immediate emergency requiring intervention before addressing the airway/breathing concern.

---

**Question 2:** When preparing to administer furosemide 40mg IV to a patient with acute decompensated heart failure, which assessment finding should cause the nurse to hold the medication and contact the prescriber?

A) Serum potassium of 3.1 mEq/L  
B) Blood pressure of 110/70 mmHg  
C) Urine output of 30 mL/hour  
D) Heart rate of 88 beats per minute

**✅ Correct Answer: A**

**Rationale:** A serum potassium of 3.1 mEq/L indicates hypokalemia. Furosemide is a loop diuretic that causes potassium wasting, and administering it to a hypokalemic patient risks dangerous cardiac dysrhythmias. The nurse should hold the medication and notify the prescriber before administration. The blood pressure (B) is acceptable, urine output (C) is low-normal but not a contraindication, and heart rate (D) is within normal limits.
