Case studies are among the most powerful learning tools in nursing education. They require you to apply theoretical knowledge to realistic patient scenarios, making clinical decisions under uncertainty — exactly as you will do as a practicing nurse. The NR507 course uses case studies to assess your ability to synthesize information from multiple body systems and implement the nursing process effectively.

### The Nursing Process in Case Study Analysis

**Assessment — Gathering and Interpreting Clinical Data**
Begin every case study by systematically reviewing all provided patient data: chief complaint, history of present illness (HPI), past medical history (PMH), vital signs, laboratory values, physical examination findings, and current medications. Create a complete clinical picture before drawing conclusions.

**Diagnosis — Identifying Priority Nursing Problems**
Using NANDA-I taxonomy, identify the patient's three priority nursing diagnoses. Remember to prioritize using Maslow's Hierarchy — physiological needs first, then safety, then psychosocial needs. Each diagnosis must be supported by the assessment data you collected.

**Planning — Establishing SMART Goals**
For each nursing diagnosis, establish at least one measurable, time-specific outcome. Goals should be patient-centered, realistic, and achievable within the timeframe of care. Example: "Patient will maintain oxygen saturation above 95% on 2L nasal cannula within 2 hours of intervention."

**Implementation — Evidence-Based Nursing Interventions**
Select interventions based on current clinical practice guidelines, nursing evidence, and patient preferences. Prioritize interventions that address the highest-acuity problems first. Include both independent nursing interventions and collaborative interventions requiring provider orders.

**Evaluation — Measuring Outcomes**
Determine whether patient outcomes were met, partially met, or not met. Revise the plan of care based on patient response and ongoing assessment findings. This cycle continues throughout the patient's care.
